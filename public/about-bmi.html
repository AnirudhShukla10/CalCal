About BMI (Body Mass Index)

Learn more about BMI and how it can be used to assess your health status.

What is BMI?

BMI is a number calculated from a person's height and weight. It applies to adult men and women. BMI is used as a screening tool to identify possible weight problems for adults.

How is BMI Calculated?

BMI is calculated using the following formula:

 BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)^2

For example, a person who weighs 70kg and is 1.75m tall has a BMI of:

 BMI = 70 / (1.75)^2 = 22.86

BMI Categories

BMI Range Category
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 - 24.9 Normal weight
25.0 - 29.9 Overweight
30.0 and above Obese

Fun Facts About BMI and Health

  • 1. BMI is not a diagnostic tool: It does not directly measure body fat, and it doesn't distinguish between lean body mass and fat.
  • 2. BMI can vary by age and gender: Younger adults generally have lower BMI compared to older adults. Also, BMI may not account for muscle mass in athletes.
  • 3. BMI helps identify health risks: High BMI can increase the risk of diseases like heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers.
  • 4. BMI is an easy, quick way to assess health: It's a useful first step in determining whether you're underweight, overweight, or at a healthy weight.
